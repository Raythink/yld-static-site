About us

Located in the beautiful Futian District of Shenzhen, China, Shenzhen Enlighten International School (SEIS) is a high-end, American international boarding school with complete facilities. It is situated at the intersection of Futian and Nanshan Districts, offering convenient transportation and a beautiful environment. The school provides first-class living services and a comfortable teaching environment, including intelligent student dormitories and 24-hour humanistic care for students.

SEIS follows the American-style education system in cooperation with the California State Education System. It undertakes the U.S. California public education standards and introduces the advanced teaching curriculum system of American public high schools, with English language instruction creating a strong American academic atmosphere. The school provides high-quality U.S. education for the children of Shenzhen and the country.

Currently, the school is enrolling students in grades 7-12.

Campus life

Students at SEIS have one hour of extracurricular activity time every afternoon. There are multiple clubs and organizations for students to choose from, including yoga, swimming, fitness, basketball, music, musical instruments, robots, etc., providing students with a space to fully showcase their talents. In addition, a variety of campus activities such as the American School Games, The Voice of England, dance competitions, drama festivals, dubbing competitions, Halloween activities, Christmas activities, etc., make students enjoy themselves.

Campus Features

  1. Practicing small class teaching (less than 20 students per class)
  2. 1:5 teacher-student ratio to create an all-round elite, so that children are no longer confused
  3. Former admissions officers of Ivy League universities, renowned instructors of international education and current teaching professionals serve as college counselors to provide comprehensive
    College planning and guidance
  4. Experienced team of college counselors strive to help all graduates enter high quality overseas universities.
  5. Strong bilingual teachers as subject assistants and homeroom teachers, and after-school tutoring for students.
  6. Mutual support channels to minimize the communication gap between teachers and students and encourage students to participate in the program.
  7. A number of well-equipped laboratories are set up to emphasize the development of students’ hands-on research skills.
  8. Parent classes are held year-round to promote parent-child communication.
  9. A transparent home-school communication platform is set up to facilitate barrier-free communication between parents and the school.
